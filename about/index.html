My Journey

My love of golf started early; I was somewhat of a child prodigy, showing a talent and passion for the game from an early age. When I was two-years old, I featured in a newspaper article in The Evening Post 2004.​

I was so passionate about golf that I practiced every day, understanding right from the start how important discipline and consistency would be. My parents have a funny story about the neighbours calling the Police because they assumed my father was purposefully continually hitting golf balls at their roof. I was more than happy to show the policemen, at age two, that it was me hitting the golf ball that far.​

A key moment which demonstrated how my natural talent and hard work was paying off, was when 11 years old I was selected to be part of the junior coaching elite squad at Miramar Golf Club.​

After being moved up to the elite squad, I trained under the golf pro, Renee Fowler, and this is where my game truly took off. I excelled quickly and was invited to play in the Miramar Men's Interclub team, an exciting recognition that provided me with a solid understanding of competitive golf.​

For the next 2 years, I played for Miramar in the Men's Interclub teams rising through the grades (divisions 8 and 6).​

When I was 13 years old, I was promoted into the Miramar Senior Men's team; an incredible leap forward, and I spent these years embracing every challenge, learning valuable lessons from my losses that fuelled my growth and reinforced my determination. Within two years, at just 15 years old, I was playing Number 1 for the Miramar Senior Men’s team. ​

After numerous caps for the Wellington Junior team, in 2019, I was 16 years old when I made it into the Wellington Senior Men’s squad. Later that year, I was named in the Wellington Interprovincial Team to play in the national tournament. ​

I have been a member of the Wellington Senior Men’s team since that time, attaining the coveted number 1 spot in 2024 and going through the tournament unbeaten.​
